Ensalada de Nopales Asados - Latino USA Episode 429

This segment is Susanna Trilling’s recipe for Ensalada de Nopales Asados, or grilled cactus salad.

Susanna Trilling is a chef, teacher, author and director of the _Seasons of My Heart_ cooking school. In the 1970s, she worked as a sous chef at Sweetish Hill and Fonda San Miguel in Austin, Texas. Then in the 1980s, Trilling was the chef-owner of two restaurants in New York City: Rick’s Lounge and Bon Temps Roller. According to her website, she also opened her catering business, Seasons of my Heart. She moved to Oaxaca, Mexico in 1988 with her family, where she opened a bed and breakfast in 1994. From her ranch just outside of the city, she began growing her own produce.

Cooking with Nopales

Nopal is the Spanish name for cacti and comes from the Nahuatl word nohpalli, meaning the “fruit of the earth.” Folks typically cut off the spines and edges before cutting them into small bite-sized pieces and boiling them to get rid of the slime. People have been eating nopal for thousands of years and it is known for being a healthy addition to any plate. Nopales are also used for other things.
